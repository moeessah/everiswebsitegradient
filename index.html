Why we exist

Five things we believe
about owning software.

Drag or click through. These are the convictions behind every decision we make.

001 / Five beliefs

Software earns trust slowly.

The reputation that makes a product worth buying took years to build. A new owner who does not understand that will unravel it faster than they could measure.

01
002 / Five beliefs

A good acquisition keeps the character.

The personality of a great product came from real decisions by real people who cared. That is not decoration. It is the product.

02
003 / Five beliefs

AI should be quiet about itself.

The best AI in a product is the kind you forget is there. Adding it so you can call it an AI product is a distraction, not an improvement.

03
004 / Five beliefs

Long horizons change what you optimize for.

If you plan to own something for a decade, you care more about whether customers are genuinely happy and less about what monthly growth looks like on a deck.

04
005 / Five beliefs

We have done this before.

Time Doctor. Over a decade. Running Remote. Real and recurring. None of this is theory. We know what it costs because we paid it.
